The Veil Unbound · Act I · Session 12

The Duskmire Sanctum

Where the rot remembers what the gods forgot
Party Level: 6  ·  Players: 5 (Za'ren Present)  ·  Tone: Dark Fantasy Horror / Heroic Epic  ·  Stakes: Campaign-Defining
Session Overview

The party — including Za'ren — wakes rested in the Shattered Spire's Sanctuary Room at Level 6, the Sundered Diamond and Ember Shard secured. They descend the tower, travel 2.5 days northwest across Velastra's open plains and into hilly terrain, and arrive at Thornmere — a hamlet on the edge of the Duskmire swamp. There they learn two strangers passed through days ago heading exactly where they are going. Inside the sinking ruins of a drowned village and its half-submerged temple, they uncover what Agares has been building toward. They will arrive, explore, be tested — and will almost certainly be too late.

Primary Goal: Reach the Duskmire Sanctum's inner chamber. Witness or interrupt Agares' ascension rite. Leave changed.

Secondary Goal: Barnabas and Fippy find threads that pull them personally into this location.

Narrative Engine: The party is always slightly behind. Every delay tightens the clock. This is cause and effect, not DM fiat.

Scene 1 — The Sanctuary Room
01
The Weight of Level Six
Roleplay / Transition
♪ Soundtrack — "Hope" · quiet, reflective morning-after

The Sanctuary Room keeps its quiet like a held breath. Bare stone walls, a floor scrubbed clean of the tower's corruption, and the low hum of a ward some long-dead hand pressed into the threshold — something that said not here to whatever moved through the floors above. You slept the whole night through, no dreams. The first true rest since you set foot on the Spire's lowest stair.

The Sundered Diamond waits in its open case, throwing off a warmth the cold rock around it has no business allowing. Beside it the Ember Shard turns its two sparks in a slow, patient orbit. Thessavra's screams have faded to memory. The tower overhead is gutted, empty, dead.

You are rested, and you are not the people who climbed in here. The work of this place has set into you the way tempering sets into a blade — and you can feel the new weight of yourselves when you stand.

DM Note — Opening Beat

Give them the moment. Let each player acknowledge the level-up however they wish in character. Za'ren is present — his psychic vulnerability from the Spire has passed with the long rest, though whatever his sister showed him in that dream lingers. The Sundered Diamond will generate immediate table conversation — three meaningful choices, mutually exclusive. Don't push them toward one. The tension is the reward.

After 10–15 minutes, move them toward descent. The air smells of rain and rot. The swamp is northwest. It has always been northwest.

The Sundered Diamond — Case Contents
The Sundered Diamond
A fist-sized raw diamond, uncut, radiating faint warmth. Ancient — locked deliberately by scholars who knew exactly what it was. Three uses, mutually exclusive:
— Sell It
Worth 1,500gp to the right buyer (arcane scholar, noble collector, major temple). ~800gp on the open market.
— Resurrection Component
Counts as the full material component for Raise Dead or Resurrection. Fully consumed on use.
— Speak with Dead (1 use)
Attune to the diamond. Cast Speak with Dead once, no components required. Diamond then goes cold and dark — retains ~700gp sale value.
Why Did the Scholars Have This?

The diamond was locked in an identified case — they knew what it was. Why did scholars working in a corrupted celestial tower have a resurrection-grade diamond under lock and key? Were they expecting casualties? Protecting something irreplaceable? You don't need to answer. But the players may ask, and the question is worth leaving open.

Table Roleplay — The Quiet Hour
This is the only unhurried hour the party gets all session. Don't rush them out of it — spend it.
Go around the table. Each player names one small, private thing their character does now that the climb is over: a wound finally tended, a whispered prayer, a letter that never got sent, a long look at someone they nearly lost up there.
Put the Sundered Diamond on the table — a token, if you've got one. Ask the question out loud: if it came to it, who would each of you bring back? Let them argue. Let them not agree.
Whoever wants the diamond has to say why, to the others' faces. Whoever doesn't want it spent has to say that too.
Scene 2 — The Descent
02
What the Tower Leaves You With
Exploration
♪ Soundtrack — "Abandoned Village" · the gutted, empty tower

The climb down through the Shattered Spire passes without a fight. The mechanical spiders lie where they fell. The floors are silent. But the tower still has things to show you on the way out — arcane residue smearing the air in certain doorways, a scholar's notes curling off a rotted corkboard.

The fourth-floor kitchen stops you. Someone set this table, once. Six plates, three chairs — they always meant to drag in more chairs — and cups gone to grey dust. A duty roster hangs on the chalkboard beside the shelf. The last entry carries a date and then breaks off mid-word, as if the writer was called away and never made it back.

Outside, the dead land around the Spire runs flat to every horizon. Northwest, the world starts to change — greener, lower, wetter. You can't see the swamp yet. You can smell it from here: standing water and slow, patient rot.

The Duty Roster

One name on the roster shares a surname with the portrait the party found — "please, for Markus, tell him I'm sorry." Pure flavor continuity. No player needs to notice. But if they do, honor it.

Scene 3 — The Road to Thornmere
03
Two and a Half Days Northwest
Travel / Decompression
♪ Soundtrack — "Meadow Campfire" · the road, the open sky, the fire

The journey from the Shattered Spire to Thornmere takes two and a half days on foot across Velastra's open interior. The landscape changes slowly: the barren dead ground around the Spire gives way to open plains, then to gently rolling hills, and finally — on the morning of the third day — to a thickening tree line at the edge of a forest that presses up against Thornmere's southern boundary. The road is old. It has seen better decades. But it holds.

There are no fights on this road. What there is: time. Space. The particular weight of carrying things you haven't yet decided what to do with.

⚠ DM Eyes Only — The Feeling of Being Watched

From the moment the party leaves the Spire, they are being watched. Not by an agent. Not by a creature. By Yssovare himself — the Veiled God, whose crystallized mortal heart the party now carries in the form of the Ember Shard. He is aware of it moving through the world. He is aware of them.

This is not a threat and not an action. It is simply attention. The kind of attention a god pays when something sacred to him is in motion. The party will feel it and will not understand it. They may never connect it to the Shard. That is correct. This secret runs long.

Do not over-describe the sensation. It should be ambient, persistent, and slightly wrong — like a sound just below hearing, or a shadow in peripheral vision that is never there when you look directly at it.

Day One — The Plains

Open sky, open ground. The Spire shrinks behind them. For the first time in days there is no ceiling pressing down. The party is free to breathe and decompress — this is the place for campfire conversation, debate about the Sundered Diamond, questions about what they saw in the Spire's deepest floors. Encourage roleplay. Let them talk about what they're carrying, literally and otherwise.

By nightfall, the first campfire. And with it — very quietly, noticed only if a player mentions it — the faint, persistent sense that the darkness just past the firelight is paying attention. Not hostile. Attentive. A DC 14 Perception check to notice it consciously. A DC 18 to feel it as something directed rather than ambient.

PERCEPTION — Something in the Dark  Passive or Active, Night Camps

Nothing. The plains are quiet. Whatever watches does so at a distance you cannot feel.
14–17
A sensation, not a sound. The darkness at the edge of the firelight feels occupied in a way you can't justify. Nothing moves. Nothing is there when you look. But the feeling doesn't leave.

Table Roleplay — Around the First Fire
The road is space the dungeon never gives them. Use the first night to let the party just be people for a while.
Open it up: what does each character carry out of the Spire that they haven't said out loud yet? Pair people who rarely talk — let two players pick a conversation and run it without you steering.
Go around once for watch order. Whoever takes the late watch sits alone with the fire — a quiet window for one player to show you something private about their character.
If a player notices the watched feeling, don't explain it. Let them raise it to the group and decide together whether it's nerves or something real. The not-knowing is the scene.
Day Two — The Hills

The plains fold upward into gentle hillocks. The road winds. The sky is overcast — not threatening, just grey and heavy. This is a good day for quieter character moments: Za'ren processing what he experienced in the Spire's psychic resonance, Barnabas moving with the deliberate quiet of someone turning something over in his mind, Clementine perhaps looking at her blue book and finding — or not finding — something she thought she remembered seeing.

The watched feeling is stronger today. Still ambient, still wordless. But persistent in the way a headache persists — noticeable precisely because it never quite goes away.

Toward afternoon: a Wild Magic moment for Corwen. Low-stakes, character-illuminating. Suggested: he reaches for something mundane — lighting a pipe, warming his hands, a small cantrip — and something else comes out instead. Brief, strange, and with the specific quality of wild magic that feels less like randomness and more like something is looking through the window of his power from the other side. Roll on a wild magic table or pick a moment that fits the scene.

Corwen's Wild Magic — Tone Note

Keep this light and grounded rather than spectacular. The point isn't a big magical event — it's a moment that reminds the table that Corwen's power has a quality that isn't entirely his own. The wild magic feels slightly watched when it fires. Just a beat. Just a texture. Justin doesn't need to understand why. The moment passes.

Day Two, Evening — The Merchant

On the downslope of the last hill before the tree line, heading the opposite direction: a merchant with a loaded mule and the careful pace of someone who has been doing this route long enough to know not to hurry. He is friendly in the way of people who travel alone and appreciate conversation. He has been through Thornmere three days ago heading south.

Oswin Tetch
Itinerant merchant, 50s, round-faced, good boots. Trades dried goods and small sundries between Velastra's interior hamlets. Has been doing this route for twenty years.
Manner: Easy, direct, no agenda. Happy to share what he knows. Won't be pushed to speculate beyond what he saw.
Rumor A: "Place has been dead quiet for years. Then three, four days back, smoke rising from the direction of the old sanctum. Not campfire smoke. The wrong color. Folks in Thornmere won't talk about it much. The woman who runs the place — Dunhollow — she was watching that smoke all morning with an expression I didn't like."
Rumor D: "Heard one of the old families there lost a son last season. Young man, went into the swamp on a dare. They found his boots. That's all they found. The swamp gives back boots, apparently. Not much else." He says this matter-of-factly, the way you report a local fact you have accepted. "I'd say stay out of that swamp, but you've got the look of people who are going to anyway."
If asked about the old sanctum: "I don't know anything about it except that it's been there longer than anyone alive and everyone in Thornmere pretends it isn't. You know how places get. Some things, you just stop looking at after a while."
If asked about the smoke: He thinks on this. "Purple-black. Like something burning that didn't want to burn. I saw it two mornings running, then it stopped. Figured whatever it was, it was done."
The Merchant — What He Tells Them

Oswin is purely informational. He has no hidden agenda. Let the party ask their questions and answer them honestly. He knows nothing about the Ordo, nothing about Agares by name, and nothing about the Counting Coven. He just moves goods and observes the world around him. His value is the clock confirmation: the smoke two mornings running that then stopped. The rite is in progress. They are behind.

When the conversation ends, he wishes them well with the specific warmth of someone who does not expect to see travelers who go toward the Duskmire come back. He continues south. He doesn't look back.

Day Three, Morning — The Forest Edge

The road enters the tree line an hour before Thornmere. The forest here is old — not the horror of the Duskmire swamp, but dense and quiet in the way old forests are quiet. Light comes through in columns. The road is mulched with decades of leaf fall. The air is cooler and smells of earth and something faintly damp.

This is where Fippy's unease begins.

⚠ Fippy — Growing Unease (Player-Specific, Subtle)

As the party enters the forest and closes the distance to Thornmere and the Duskmire beyond, Fippy begins to feel something he cannot name. It is not fear exactly. It is more like the particular discomfort of a memory that won't surface — something that wants to be recognized and can't quite be.

Source — both things at once: His brand is responding to proximity to long-standing coven activity in this region. The Counting Coven has had a watch on the Duskmire Sanctum for generations. The runic geometry of his brand is, at some deep level, attuned to the coven's presence. He is getting closer to something that his brand knows even when he doesn't.

How to play it: Don't announce this to the player directly. Describe it physically — a faint prickling where the brand sits, a restlessness he can't account for, the specific sensation of being slightly too aware of his own skin. If Joshi asks what his character feels, give him the sensation without the explanation. He doesn't know why. That is correct.

It is not dangerous. It is not a warning. It is recognition without context. The answer to what it means is in a collapsed side chapel ahead of him, carved into a dead person's wrist bone.

FIPPY — Arcana or Nature (Instinct Check, Forest Edge)  DC 12, Unsolicited
1–11
The forest feels normal. Whatever is unsettling him has no name he can attach to it.
12–15
The feeling is coming from somewhere ahead, not from around him. It isn't the forest. It's something beyond the forest that the forest is adjacent to.
16–20

Arriving at Thornmere

The forest opens. The hamlet resolves from the tree line — twelve buildings, a well, a stubbornness. Beyond it, the landscape softens and darkens. The trees thin into something wetter. The smell changes. They have arrived.

Scene 4 — Thornmere
04
The Hamlet at the Edge of the Rot
Social
♪ Soundtrack — "Isolated Village" · wary Thornmere

Thornmere is not quite a village. It's twelve buildings, a well, and a stubbornness. These people have lived on the edge of the Duskmire for three generations. They are not afraid of it — they are careful of it, the way you are careful of a neighbour whose dog bites. They are suspicious of travellers who ask about the Sanctum, because travellers who ask about the Sanctum always mean trouble.

Thornmere announces itself as a smell first — woodsmoke, and under it something sour and fermenting. Then the buildings resolve from the tree line: low structures, grey-thatched and practical, hunched at the edge of ground that cooperates a little less with every step. The road ends here. Past the last building the earth softens, the trees thicken, and the light goes thin and grey.

A woman is splitting wood near the well. She stops when she sees you. She doesn't run, doesn't call out — she just watches, axe loose in one hand, with the flat patience of someone who got over being frightened of things a long while ago and now simply takes their measure.

Behind her, a deep herb garden sprawls along the south wall of the cottage — a dense, half-wild tangle of plants, vegetables, and stranger things you'd have to crouch down to name. The garden of someone who has been growing things for forty years and long since stopped arranging them for any eye but her own.

Maret Dunhollow
Thornmere's self-appointed caretaker. 60s, Velastra human. Permanent scowl of someone who has seen enough.
Voice: Economical. Minimum words. Silence is a tool. She releases information in exchange for something that seems worth it.
What she knows: "Two strangers, four days ago. One was tall, impeccably dressed — had the kind of smile that means something is wrong with the teeth behind it. He wore red. His companion didn't speak. They asked about the old path to the Sanctum. I told them there was no safe path. They laughed. They left."
On the Sanctum: "That place has been sinking for as long as my grandmother's grandmother could remember. The village that used to be there — we don't speak its name. The people who built that temple thought they'd found something worth worshipping. They found something, all right."
On the old village: "The priests changed first. Started talking about the worship differently. Not the god they'd always served — something older, something that was watching before the gods had names for watching. The village followed because villages follow priests. The swamp followed because the swamp follows everything."
What she gives: Directions to the firm path. A warning: "If you hear singing — and you might — don't follow it. And don't answer back." She does not explain further.
INSIGHT — Reading Maret  DC 13
1–5
She seems hostile and deliberately unhelpful. You're wasting your time here.
6–10
She's not hostile — she's cautious. She's seen what happens to people who go to the Sanctum and she doesn't want to feel responsible for more of it.
11–15
She knows more than she's saying about the two strangers. The description of the tall man in red made her uncomfortable in a way she covered quickly.
16–20
She's afraid — not of you, but of what is happening at the Sanctum right now. She can feel it. People who live on the edge of something wrong for three generations develop instincts. Something has changed in the swamp in the past few days.
21+
She has been to the Sanctum. Once. Young. She has never told anyone what she saw. She will not tell you now, but you know the question exists.
Barnabas Hook: A worn symbol carved above Maret's door — a stylized hand with two fingers raised. Barnabas recognizes it immediately as an Ordo ward marker. Someone from his order passed through here, possibly years ago, and left a sign. Maret, if asked: "Big fellow. Very serious. Said he was hunting something in the swamp. He didn't come back." She says this with the blankness of someone who expected it.
Fippy Hook (Subtle): Among the dozens of herbs and vegetables in Maret's garden, Fippy spots one specific plant near the fence line. It has no practical use outside of coven binding rituals. Not alarming on its own — it grows wild in swamp country. But Fippy knows why someone would cultivate it deliberately, and this woman has clearly been growing it for years. If asked, Maret says a woman whose name she never knew paid her to keep a patch going. Years back. Never explained why.
Table Roleplay — Earning Maret's Words
Maret gives nothing for free. Make the party work the conversation, not the dice.
She answers in trade, not charity. Before she'll talk about the strangers in red, ask each player what their character actually offers — coin, a day's labour, news from the road, an honest answer to a question of her own.
The ward marker over her door is Barnabas's to raise. Let that player choose: ask in front of everyone, or hold it back? It's an order matter. Make sharing it cost something.
If the party gets grabby or rude, she simply stops talking and goes back to her wood. Reward patience here, not pressure.
Scene 5 — The Duskmire Approach
05
Through the Swamp
Travel / Hazard
♪ Soundtrack — "Dark Swamp" · the Duskmire approach

The path Maret described holds for the first hour. After that it turns into a suggestion, and then into something you keep walking only because you've decided to call it a path. Water climbs the roots of trees that have been dead so long they've forgotten what killed them.

The smell deepens — thick, organic, wrong. Not the sharp reek of fresh rot but something settled and ancient, the air of a place where something terrible happened a very long time ago and the land never closed over the wound.

Then the buildings begin. Or what the swamp left of them. Half-submerged stone walls breaking the water. A chimney standing alone with no house left around it. A doorframe rooted in black water, opening onto nothing, still upright as though it expects the rest of the house to come back for it. This village didn't burn. It drowned — slowly, inch by inch, year by year, until the swamp closed over the last roof like a mouth.

Swamp Hazard — Rot Sickness: Run this check twice — once entering the village ruins, once entering the Sanctum. Each PC makes a DC 13 Constitution saving throw. Failure: Rot Fatigue — disadvantage on Constitution saving throws until a short rest outside the swamp or magical healing. This stacks.

Scene 6 — The Upper Sanctum
06
What Agares Left Behind
Exploration / Discovery
♪ Soundtrack — "Dungeon" · the living, watching Sanctum

The Sanctum entrance is a broad stone arch, half of it underwater. Inside, the walls are old cut stone, smooth in the way that takes generations of devotion to achieve. Someone cared about this place once. You can see that care in the craftsmanship even as the corruption has consumed it — beautiful stonework covered in a creeping black mold that moves, almost imperceptibly, at the edge of your torchlight.

There is something on the ground ahead. Fabric. Red. A litham — a face-covering worn in certain traditions — and it has been torn, not removed. As if pulled away in haste or desperation.

It is made of the same material as the one Barnabas carries.

Barnabas — The Litham: He recognizes it immediately. The material, the cut, the way the ties are knotted — this is Ordo-issue. It belongs to one of his order. He does not know whose. Combined with the ward marker at Maret's door: someone from the Ordo came here, left a warning at the hamlet, entered the Sanctum, and did not come back. The litham was torn. There is no body.
Table Roleplay — A Brother's Cloth
Two hooks have now landed on Barnabas — the ward marker, and the torn red litham. Let it land in the room before the dice come back out.
Hand the litham to Barnabas's player and let them describe what their character does with it. Pocket it? Hold it up for the others? Say a word over it?
Invite one other PC to ask Barnabas, in character, what the Ordo actually is and why one of them would die out here. Let Barnabas decide how much truth to give.
Keep it short and let the silence sit. From here on in, the party is walking somebody's last footsteps.

⚠ Fippy — Easter Egg (Deliver Privately)

Location: A collapsed side chapel off the main corridor. DC 11 Perception to notice the partially-blocked archway. Inside: stone benches, some toppled. Centuries-old bones.

One set of remains sits slumped in the corner — small bones, halfling-sized. On the wrist bone, barely visible beneath centuries of grime, a brand burned so deeply into the bone it survived the decay of everything else.

The brand: Fippy recognizes the geometry immediately. Same runic family as his own mark. Same craft. A different number. This person bore a different brand from the same coven. They died here, alone, centuries ago. No explanation. No ghost. No journal. Just the bone and the brand and the knowledge that the coven's reach goes back further than he thought — into places and purposes he has no context for yet.

Pass a note or a quiet word. This is his discovery to keep or share.

Scene 7 — The Deep Sanctum
07
The Inner Chamber
Climax
♪ Boss — "Mage Boss Fight" · the Cambion & the rite (kill it before the seven words)

The stairs descend below the water table into chambers that should be flooded but are not — the ritual has pushed the water back. The air is warm and wrong. It smells of sulfur and something older — iron and incense and the specific smell of a fire that has been burning for a very long time.

The stairs end at an archway. And the archway opens into the heart of it.

The inner chamber is circular, perhaps sixty feet across, the ceiling vaulting upward into darkness. The floor is the original temple stone — ancient, worn smooth by centuries of kneeling — and carved into it, covering every inch, a ritual circle of extraordinary complexity. Hundreds of interlocking sigils. The geometry of something that took generations to perfect.

In the center of that circle stands a man you recognize.

He is not wearing red. He stands barefoot in robes of deep charcoal grey, arms slightly spread, eyes closed. The air around him moves without wind — slow currents of something invisible rotating around his form. At the four cardinal points, iron stands hold bowls of burning black flame. No heat comes from them. The flames exist but do not warm.

His companion — a figure in grey, featureless, whose footsteps make no sound — stands at the circle's edge. Facing outward. Facing you.

⚠ DM Eyes Only — The Full Picture

Agares knows the party is here. He knew before they entered. He is approximately 15 minutes from completion when they arrive. This is the "too late" default.

His companion is a Cambion — half-fiend servant. Its function: delay. It will fight, negotiate, threaten, and buy time. It is not stupid.

Five-Fallow Naldri has been scrying this location via the coven's established generational watch. She observes. When Agares ascends, the power surge snaps her scry connection. She has what she needs. The coven now knows.

The Timing Track — Three Outcomes:

Early Arrival

The rite is interruptible. Dispatching the Cambion quickly and disrupting the circle (DC 16 Arcana as an action, destroying one iron stand) collapses the rite. Agares is expelled, fights unstably, escapes at 30% HP. A genuine win — at enormous cost.

On Time

The rite is at critical inflection. Agares fights while maintaining it. He achieves partial ascension — half the power, all the consequences. He leaves changed but not fully elevated.

Default — Too Late

The rite completes. Agares ascends. He turns to look at all of them at once. He does not kill them. He says seven words and leaves. This is the intended outcome.

With five PCs including Za'ren, this encounter will move faster — adjust the countdown down by 1 round if the party looks likely to overwhelm it quickly.

The Cambion steps aside. Or drops. Or bolts. It doesn't matter which.

You are too late by the length of a single breath.

The circle catches — not with fire, but with meaning. Every sigil flares at once, every carved line of darkness snapping bright, and for one instant the whole pattern reads as a single word your mind refuses to keep hold of. The chamber makes a sound that isn't quite a sound — a pressure behind the teeth, a silence with weight to it, the held pause at the very bottom of a breath.

Agares opens his eyes.

They are darker than they were, and deeper, and there is something behind them now that wasn't there when you climbed down — something old and unhurried, settling in like a tenant taking a room it has waited centuries to claim.

He lowers his arms. He breathes once, slow. Then he turns and looks at you — at every one of you at the same time, his gaze finding all five faces at once though his head never turns that far. What's in his face isn't triumph. It's quieter than that. Recognition. The calm of a man watching a thing he predicted come true, dead on schedule.

He says seven words.

♪ Cut the music — stop the track here. Deliver the seven words into total silence.
⚠ The Seven Words — Deliver Quietly

Not a threat. Not a boast. A statement of fact from something that has ceased to need either. His voice carries slightly more than it should.

"You were always meant to witness this."

Then he is gone. Not teleported — teleporting takes effort, and effort is beneath what he's become. He is simply here, and then he is simply not. The circle's glow fades. The black flames extinguish. The chamber goes cold and quiet.

Scratched into the floor at the center of the circle, in still-warm stone: a partial symbol in a dead liturgical script. The party cannot read it. But Clementine, looking at one specific sequence of characters, feels a cold recognition she cannot explain. She has seen three of those letters before. She does not know where. (DM: it is from her blue book's glowing script, Session 5. She has been carrying a fragment of Yssovare's mortal name without knowing it.)

Scene 8 — What the Sanctum Leaves You With
08
Aftermath in the Deep
Denouement / Discovery
♪ Soundtrack — "Battle Aftermath" · the cold, emptied chamber

The chamber is cold now. The warmth that the rite generated has gone with the man who generated it. The water that the ritual pushed back has begun, slowly, to seep through the walls again. You have perhaps an hour before the deep sanctum begins to flood.

But the chamber offers what it always has — its history. The walls down here are the oldest in the Sanctum. And here, in the oldest layer, the eye behind the veil is not a symbol. It is a record. Story panels, carved in sequence, worn smooth in places but legible if you look carefully, depicting something that happened in this room a very long time ago.

Maret doesn't ask what happened. She looks at you the way people who have watched travellers go into the swamp and come back look at travellers who have gone into the swamp and come back. She steps aside. There is a fire going. There is food. She sets more plates without being asked.
